## Task (verbatim from the issue body)

## Context
Recurring human feedback on issues/PRs: condense, cut fluff, don't repeat the
diff. Re-typed every time instead of living anywhere (surfaced in #22 review).
Broadened 2026-07-20: the rule applies to **everything we generate** — chat,
commit messages, PR bodies, issues — not just issues/PRs. Global CLAUDE.md
already says "be concise" and it's ignored, so the fix is a **specific,
self-checkable shape**, not another soft "be concise."

## Scope
Add a `## Writing style` section to CLAUDE.md (loaded every session → shapes local
*and* async-Action output). Concrete, falsifiable rules:

- **Global:** fewer words wins; don't spell out unless asked; no preamble, no
  restating what's visible, no "Summary/Overview" throat-clearing.
- **Chat:** lead with the answer; group, don't enumerate; levity/bluntness fine.
- **Commit subject:** `type(#n): imperative, lowercase, ≤70 chars`; subject does
  the work.
- **Commit body:** why not what; ≤3 lines; omit when the subject suffices.
- **PR body:** what changed + why; ≤5 lines unless asked; no `## Summary`, no diff
  restatement, no checklist theater; link the issue.
- **Issue body:** problem + shape of done; durable guidance only (§2 snapshot
  excludes comments); tight.
- **Self-check before send:** could this be half the length without losing signal?
  If yes, cut.
- **Templates:** fill `.github/ISSUE_TEMPLATE/` skeletons, don't hand-roll.
